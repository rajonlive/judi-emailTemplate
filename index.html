Here’s the thing about
human beings in general

WE'RE NEVER READY - we’re never ready to break through to new horizons, to see hope beyond hopelessness nor resounding courage beyond fear. Take our NYE resolutions that in essence turn out to be merely wishful thinking; those once a year promises we make at midnight to HOPEFULLY change the things about ourself that disappoint us the most.

WE RESIST CHANGE OF ANY TYPE - and most often to our own detriment. Out of FEAR we refuse to acknowledge CHANGE as being a builder of inner strength, emotional resilience and growth on so many levels.

WE RELY ON COMFORT - Those deeply impregnated patterns of behaviour that in most instances provide only temporary and feel-good relief rather than any serious commitment on our part to long term change. And here’s the kicker;

WE STRUGGLE WITH HONESTY - being really honest with ourselves is overwhelming, so confronting and absolutely detrimental to the stability of our emotional walls of defence or armour plating if you will. Yet, it is those seemingly impenetrable walls of protection that need to tumble one by one for real and lasting change to be effected.

But at some point . . .

I WAS 54 YEARS OLD - before I STARTED MY JOURNEY OF DISCOVERY never knowing how long that journey would take me let alone, knowing if I would have the fortitude to see it through. Hence the title of my book:
WHEN TIME MEANS NOTHING

After so many long, draining and emotional years in fact 17 years and four months of endless soul searching, marking time and mental gymnastics resulting from events and discoveries about myself and history regarding my past, I find myself left only with questions:

  • WHAT HAPPENED, why has it taken forever to complete my Journey of Self-Development, and CRUCIALLY, what can I honestly say I HAVE LEARNED from my experience?
  • The truth is, I haven’t completed my journey at all. It is far from over!
